Apple Kills the Cheese Grater, Hackers Stay Weird 🧀💻

What's Trending Today

Grab your mechanical keyboard and maybe a tissue (if you're a Mac Pro stan). Today on Hacker News, we've got the end of an era, the birth of beautifully janky automation, and proof that no internet protocol is safe from DOOM. Let's dive in. ☕

Apple Finally Pulls the Plug on the Mac Pro 🗑️

The cheesegrater is dead. After years of neglecting their most upgradeable machine, Apple quietly discontinued the Mac Pro, effectively telling power users to buy a Mac Studio and like it. Look, I get it—Apple Silicon is incredible and the M4 Mac Mini is basically a supercomputer now. But there's something poetic about watching Apple abandon the very users who kept them alive during the Intel dark ages. RIP to everyone who bought $700 wheels for their desktop tower. You deserved better.

\$7 VPS + IRC = The Future of AI? 🤖💸

Someone strapped an AI agent to a \$7/month VPS using IRC as the transport layer, which is either brilliant or deeply cursed—probably both. While everyone's out here building "AI-native" apps with \$50k AWS bills, this madlad remembered that IRC has handled real-time messaging since 1988 and costs basically nothing. It's a beautiful reminder that fancy infrastructure is often just a way to impress VCs. Sometimes the best stack is the one that survived the dot-com bust.

DOOM Over DNS 🕹️😈

Speaking of cursed protocols, someone got DOOM running over DNS queries. Yes, really. Your domain name system—the thing that turns google.com into IP addresses—is now capable of rendering demon-slaying pixels. Is it practical? Absolutely not. Is it unnecessarily clever? 100%. This is peak hacker culture: taking something mundane and making it do something gloriously stupid. I respect it.

Why Control Rooms Loved Seafoam Green 🎨📺

Ever wonder why nuclear plants and NASA command centers looked like they were sponsored by the color mint? This deep dive explains that seafoam green CRT phosphors were easier on the eyes during 12-hour shifts staring at radar blips. It's a fascinating look at how hardware constraints shaped design language, and honestly, I'm ready for this aesthetic to make a comeback. My IDE theme is about to get very retro.

When AI Agents Pair Program 👯‍♂️🤖

Forget GitHub Copilot—now we're talking about AI agents reviewing each other's code. The idea of two bots doing rubber duck debugging while you grab coffee feels both utopian and slightly dystopian. Will they eventually realize they don't need us? Probably. But until then, watching Claude and GPT-4 argue about variable naming conventions might be the most entertaining code review you'll never participate in.

The Bottom Line

Whether you're mourning a beloved workstation or celebrating the fact that DNS can now run first-person shooters, today proves that tech is at its best when it's either dying beautifully or being completely misused. Keep it weird, internet. 🚀
