Tanner's Tech News - 2026-03-27

"2026-03-27"

From Bedroom Bots to Book Bans: Tech's Identity Crisis 🎭

Today's tech landscape is serving pure chaos: OpenAI is killing off spicy chatbots, Apple's playing Big Brother with your birthdate, and somewhere a librarian is screaming into the void as AI quietly shelves 1984 alongside vampire romance novels. Grab your overpriced coffee—we're diving into the mess.

OpenAI's Erotic Retreat 🚫💋

OpenAI officially pulled the plug on ChatGPT's "erotic mode" (which apparently existed? did anyone beta test this?). Just when we thought AI couldn't get any more prudish, they're abandoning what they called a "side quest"—probably wise given the headlines, but let's be honest, half of you are just mad you didn't get to try it before the moral panic set in. RIP to the digital romance no one asked for but apparently many attempted.

Netflix Hits Us Where It Hurts 📺💸

Speaking of things that disappoint, Netflix confirmed another price hike—their second this year. At this point, we're not streaming content, we're funding corporate debt. Between password crackdowns and this, the "Netflix and chill" era is officially dead; welcome to "Netflix and calculate if rent can wait." Time to dust off that library card... unless the AI got there first.

iPhone Users Threaten the Green Bubble 📱😤

iOS is pushing mandatory age verification and iPhone users are threatening to defect to Android en masse. Look, Apple, we get you want to be the "privacy company," but asking adults to prove they're adults to use their $1,200 pocket computers is peak surveillance capitalism. The exodus threat is real, though let's see how many actually switch when they realize green bubbles and chaotic group chats await.

AMD's Cache Monster Enters the Chat 🧠⚡

AMD dropped the Ryzen 9 9950X3D2 with a ridiculous 208MB of cache crammed onto one chip. That's not a processor, that's a digital hoarder's dream apartment. For context, that's more cache than my brain has available before noon. If you're wondering why you should care: this chip is basically cheating at video games. Intel's "conditionally great" CPUs (their words, not mine) just got conditionally obsolete.

When AI Burns Books—Literally 1984 📚🤖

A librarian was "gobsmacked" (perfect British understatement) after school AI systems removed 200 books including Orwell's 1984 and Twilight. The irony of an algorithm removing 1984 is too on-the-nose—it feels like a plant by the marketing team for the sequel nobody wants. When the machines start banning dystopian fiction about machines banning things, maybe we should... read the room?

Anthropic Actually Fights the Power 🏛️⚖️

On the serious tip: Anthropic just won an injunction against the Trump administration over Defense Department contracts. While everyone else is playing with erotic chatbots, Anthropic is actually battling government overreach in AI deployment. It's refreshing to see someone with "AI" in their name focused on accountability rather than just harvesting your data to sell you shoes.

Whether it's chips with more memory than your first computer had storage, or algorithms with worse literary taste than a middle schooler, today proves tech is just humanity's neuroses written in Python and silicon. Stay skeptical, clear your cookies, and maybe buy that external hard drive before the router ban hits. 🔧
